# Vietnamese Cuisine

## Pho

Pho is Vietnam's most traditional and famous dish, especially Hanoi Pho. The dish consists of soft rice noodles, clear broth, beef or chicken, and fresh herbs like green onions, cilantro, and bean sprouts. Pho originated in early 20th century Hanoi, influenced by French culinary culture. The secret to a delicious bowl of pho is the broth, simmered from bones for many hours with spices like star anise, cinnamon, and ginger. Hanoians typically eat pho for breakfast. A bowl of pho costs between 30,000 to 60,000 VND. Famous pho restaurants in Hanoi include Pho Thin, Pho Bat Dan, and Pho 10 Ly Quoc Su.

## Bun Cha

Bun cha is a signature Hanoi dish consisting of fresh rice vermicelli, grilled pork patties (cha com and cha mieng), sweet and sour fish sauce, and fresh vegetables. The pork is grilled over charcoal creating a distinctive aroma. This dish became famous after U.S. President Barack Obama and chef Anthony Bourdain dined together at Bun Cha Huong Lien restaurant in 2016. Bun cha is typically served with spring rolls (nem ran or cha gio) and fresh herbs like mint, lettuce, and fish mint. A serving of bun cha costs about 40,000-60,000 VND. Famous restaurants: Bun Cha Dac Kim, Bun Cha Huong Lien, Bun Cha 34 Hang Than.

## Banh Mi

Vietnamese banh mi is a unique fusion of French baguette and Vietnamese fillings. The bread has a crispy crust, soft interior, filled with pate, Vietnamese pork sausage, cold cuts, pickled vegetables, cilantro, and chili. This dish has been voted by CNN as one of the world's best street foods. Saigon banh mi (Ho Chi Minh City) usually has more abundant fillings, while Hoi An banh mi features distinctive grilled pork skewers. A banh mi costs from 15,000-30,000 VND. Banh Mi Huynh Hoa in Saigon and Banh Mi Phuong in Hoi An are famous locations. The perfect banh mi has the right balance of savory, sweet, sour, and spicy flavors.

## Bun Bo Hue

Bun bo Hue is a traditional dish from the ancient capital of Hue, Central Vietnam. Its distinctive feature is the orange-red broth from lemongrass and fermented shrimp paste, with a spicy, aromatic flavor. Bun bo includes thicker noodles than pho, beef, Vietnamese pork sausage, and pork knuckle. This dish reflects Hue royal cuisine with complex, elaborate flavors. Typically served with fresh vegetables, bean sprouts, lime leaves, and chili. A bowl of bun bo Hue costs from 30,000-50,000 VND. To enjoy bun bo Hue properly, you should add a little sa te (chili oil) and lime juice.

## Egg Coffee

Egg coffee is Hanoi's unique beverage, invented in the 1940s when milk was scarce. The drink consists of strong drip coffee topped with a fluffy egg cream made from fresh egg yolks beaten with sugar and condensed milk. When drinking, the rich creaminess of the egg cream blends with the bitter coffee creating a unique experience. Cafe Giang (39 Nguyen Huu Huan) is the birthplace of egg coffee. A cup of egg coffee costs about 30,000-45,000 VND. Besides egg coffee, there's also an egg cocoa variant which is equally delicious. This drink has become an iconic symbol of Hanoi's cafe culture.

## Goi Cuon (Fresh Spring Rolls)

Goi cuon, or nem cuon, is a popular appetizer in Southern Vietnam. The dish consists of rice paper wrapping shrimp, boiled pork, fresh vermicelli, and raw vegetables like lettuce, mint, and onions. Served with special dipping sauce, either fish sauce or peanut sauce. Fresh spring rolls are light, refreshing, nutritious, and low in calories. This is also the most beloved Vietnamese dish abroad, known as "Vietnamese spring rolls". A plate of goi cuon (2 rolls) costs about 20,000-30,000 VND. They're healthy, easy to eat, and perfect for hot weather.

## Cao Lau

Cao lau is Hoi An's specialty dish, not found anywhere else. The special feature is the thick, chewy noodles made with water from the ancient Ba Le well. The dish includes noodles, char siu pork, raw vegetables, crispy rice crackers, and thick soy sauce. Cao lau is influenced by Chinese and Japanese cuisine, creating a unique flavor. To enjoy the best cao lau, you should visit Hoi An and try it at traditional family restaurants. A bowl of cao lau costs about 30,000-40,000 VND. The dish represents Hoi An's multicultural culinary heritage.

## Cha Ca La Vong

Cha ca La Vong is a traditional Hanoi dish over 100 years old. The dish consists of catfish or snakehead fish cut into pieces, marinated with turmeric and spices, pan-fried, served with fresh vermicelli, dill, green onions, roasted peanuts, and shrimp paste. The name comes from the famous Cha Ca La Vong restaurant on Doan Street, Ba Dinh District, Hanoi. This dish was once associated with secret meetings of patriots during French colonial times. A serving of cha ca costs about 150,000-200,000 VND. The dish is cooked at your table, creating an interactive dining experience.

## Com Tam (Broken Rice)

Com tam is a famous popular dish from Saigon. The rice is cooked from broken rice grains, served with grilled pork ribs, bi (shredded pork skin), egg patty, fried shallots, and sweet and sour fish sauce. The dish was originally peasant food but has now become a specialty loved by many. Saigon com tam restaurants usually open from early morning until late night. A plate of com tam suon costs about 30,000-50,000 VND. Com Tam Phu Lam and Com Tam My Khanh are famous addresses. It's the ultimate comfort food for locals and increasingly popular with tourists.
